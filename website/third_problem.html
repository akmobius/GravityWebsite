Ouside of the Earth

Let’s keep thinking about that rocket. How would it move if it turned off its engines right after it got out of Earth’s atmosphere (into space)? Write down your guess below (Would it move in a straight line? A circle?)

In order to understand this better, let’s clarify a few concepts.

Any two objects have a gravitational attraction between them. You and your computer have a gravitational interaction - you and your computer just have very little mass so this attraction doesn’t do much! But, as you walk away from your computer, the gravitational attraction between you and your computer gets even smaller. This is why we aren’t pulled off the Earth towards the sun. Even though the sun has more mass than the Earth, it’s so far away that the gravitational attraction between us and the Earth is much bigger than the gravitational attraction between us and the sun.

On Earth, in order to keep flying forward, an airplane has to keep its engines running at all times. This is because of air resistance. However, in space there is no air. This means that if a rocket turns off its engines, it won’t speed up anymore… but it won’t slow down either- there’s no air making it slow down! So if a rocket turns off its engines once it is in space, it will keep moving at the same speed it was before the engines were turned off.

Now let’s get back to the original question. What happens when the rocket turns off its engines right after it gets into space?

When the rocket turns off its engines, it stops pushing against the Earth’s gravity.

Because Earth’s gravity wants to pull the rocket back down to Earth, it begins to fall. However, it’s still moving very fast (remember- no air resistance in space)! Since it’s moving so fast, it misses the Earth. This happens over and over again - gravity keeps trying to pull the rocket down, but because the rocket is moving fast it keeps missing the Earth. This causes the rocket to start orbiting the Earth, kind of like the moon.
